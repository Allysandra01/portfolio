DICT Bootcamp Code of Conduct
Document ID: DICT-COC-2026-02
Effective Date: 2026-05-01
Owner: DICT Human Resources Office

1. Purpose

This code defines the behaviour expected of every participant, facilitator,
and observer in the AI-Native Productivity and Automation Bootcamp. It
applies on premises, in official chat channels, and in any recorded session.

2. Expected behaviour

2.1 Treat every participant with respect regardless of role, region, agency,
or technical background.
2.2 Listen first. Critique the work, never the person.
2.3 Credit sources. If an AI tool contributed to a deliverable, name the
tool and the model in the README.
2.4 Show your work. Repositories must include the prompts that produced the
output, not just the polished result.

3. Unacceptable behaviour

3.1 Harassment of any kind, including verbal, written, or physical.
3.2 Sharing another participant's prompt, code, or design without their
consent.
3.3 Submitting work generated by a tool or person other than the listed
contributors.
3.4 Bypassing the human-in-the-loop controls required by the automation
policy.

4. Reporting

4.1 Incidents may be reported to the facilitator in person, by email, or
through the anonymous form linked in the cohort channel.
4.2 The facilitator must acknowledge a report within 24 hours.
4.3 Retaliation against a reporter is itself a violation.

5. Consequences

5.1 First violation: documented warning and a one-on-one conversation with
the facilitator.
5.2 Second violation: removal from the cohort for the remainder of the day.
5.3 Third violation: removal from the bootcamp with notification to the
participant's home agency.

6. Review

This code is reviewed annually by the HR Office in consultation with the
cohort representatives. Material changes are communicated at least two
weeks before they take effect.
