## What is the AI-Native Productivity and Automation Bootcamp?

A five-day, in-person programme run by the Department of Information and
Communications Technology (DICT) for government employees and accredited
partners. Days 1 and 2 focus on AI productivity with cloud tools. Days 3
to 5 focus on AI automation, offline deployment, and decision support. The
final day is the Shark Tank capstone.

## Who is the bootcamp for?

DICT personnel and accredited partner agencies. Participants should be
comfortable with a terminal, basic Git, and reading or writing JavaScript
or Python. Participants do not need prior experience with AI tools.

## Where is the bootcamp held?

DICT Headquarters, Quezon City. Exact room is posted in the cohort channel
on the morning of each day. The room has Wi-Fi for Days 1 to 3 and 5. Day 4
is offline by design.

## When does the bootcamp run?

The 2026 cohort runs from 2026-05-04 to 2026-05-08, 09:00 to 17:00 each day.
Lunch is 12:00 to 13:00. There are two fifteen-minute breaks per day at
10:00 and 14:30.

## What do I need to bring?

A laptop with at least 8 GB of RAM, 20 GB of free storage, and a modern
operating system. Power adapter. Government-issued ID. The cohort channel
posts the full setup checklist one week before Day 1.

## What tools will I install?

Git, Node 20 or newer, Ollama or LM Studio with Gemma 4, OpenCode CLI and
Desktop, Google AI Studio, and Google Stitch. Setup takes about 60 minutes
for participants who already have Git and Node installed.

## Will my work be public?

Repositories are public by default on the cohort GitHub organisation. You
may make your repository private at any time, but the deliverables must be
shared with the facilitator for assessment.

## How is the bootcamp assessed?

Each day has a weight in the final grade: Day 1 (15 percent), Day 2
(15 percent), Day 3 (20 percent), Day 4 (20 percent), Day 5 Shark Tank
(30 percent). Day 5 is mandatory to pass the bootcamp.

## Can I use a different AI tool?

Yes, for ideation. The bootcamp deliverables must be reproducible on
Ollama or LM Studio with Gemma 4 to count for assessment. If you choose a
different tool, document the trade-off in your README.

## What happens after the bootcamp?

Graduates join the DICT AI Productivity alumni channel. Top capstone
projects are invited to a one-week residency to harden the prototype.
Alumni may also volunteer to mentor the next cohort.
